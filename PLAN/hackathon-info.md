# Build with AI Hackathon (University of Utah)

Source: https://ai.utah.edu/blog/posts/2026/build_with_ai_hackathon.php  
Article title: "Turn Ideas into Impact: Join the Build with AI Hackathon"  
Published: March 18, 2026  
Author: Callie Reed

## Event Overview

- AI Codex Hackathon hosted in collaboration with OpenAI.
- Virtual event on **Friday, April 17, 2026, 12:00 PM-5:00 PM (MT)**.
- Goal: help participants apply AI to real-world challenges in any discipline.
- Intended for both technical and non-technical participants, including beginners.

## Theme

**AI for Every Discipline**

Participants identify a problem in their academic/professional environment and prototype an AI-enabled solution.

## Platform / Tools

- Uses university-provided **ChatGPT Edu** with access to **Codex**, advanced features, and reasoning models.
- Participants are encouraged to activate ChatGPT Edu accounts before the event.
- Getting started link: https://ai.utah.edu/tools/chatgpt/get-started.php

## Event Format

- Virtual on Zoom.
- Includes kickoff tips/best practices, dedicated build time, and ongoing support.
- During 2:00-5:00 PM, optional 15-minute Zoom sessions with OpenAI are available for Q&A/support.

## Agenda (April 17)

- **12:00 PM** - Kickoff, welcome, introductions
- **12:20 PM** - Best practices and demonstration
- **12:50 PM** - Team formation and breakout setup
- **1:20 PM** - Team intake form completion (team name, members, project idea)
- **1:45 PM** - Hacking begins
- **2:00-5:00 PM** - Open build time + optional OpenAI support sessions
- **5:00 PM** - Day one closes; teams can continue asynchronously

## In-Person Workspace Option

- Optional in-person work available at **Adobe Creative Commons in Kahlert Village**.
- Provides workspaces, monitors, and collaboration suites.

## Challenge Tracks

1. **AI for Productivity & Workflow** - efficiency, automation, decision support
2. **AI for Social Impact** - healthcare, education, accessibility, environment, communities
3. **AI for Business & Innovation** - new products/services, operations improvements
4. **AI for Creativity & Communication** - storytelling, design, content creation, human-AI collaboration
5. **Open Track (Wildcard)** - any meaningful AI application

## Team Format

- Recommended team size: **3-5 participants**.
- Teams can be formed ahead of time or during kickoff.
- Solo participation is allowed.

## Submission Requirements

Teams submit a brief summary and supporting materials.

Required items:

- Team name
- Project title
- Problem statement
- Project description
- Demo video
- GitHub repository link (if available)
- Additional resources/supporting materials

## Timeline / Deadlines

- Day one event: **Friday, April 17, 2026**.
- Teams may continue remotely until **Saturday, April 18, 2026 at 12:00 PM MT**.
- Selected teams advance to a finalist presentation round/showcase.

## Prizes

- Event swag for winning teams.
- Top 3 teams receive OpenAI AI development credits (**not applicable to health sciences participants**).

## Important Considerations

- Projects should use only **public data** (reference: https://regulations.utah.edu/it/rules/Rule4-004C.php).
- Teams may have opportunities to share work publicly; standout projects may be highlighted beyond the event.

## Participation Benefits

- Hands-on experience using AI tools and Codex
- Real project build in a short timeframe
- Cross-disciplinary collaboration
- Opportunity to showcase ideas

## Who Should Participate

- Everyone (technical and non-technical)
- Students
- Staff
- Faculty
- Researchers
