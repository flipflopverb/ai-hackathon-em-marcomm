# University of Utah Enrollment Campaign Strategy Guidelines

## Source

- Primary source: `https://live.standards.site/universityofutah/strategy`
- Scope: distilled from the live "University of Utah Enrollment Brand" strategy and design standards content.
- Use this file as the working reference for campaign writing and design direction tasks.

## Strategic Foundation

- Anchor all communications to the enrollment platform idea: **Geared to Rise**.
- Express upward momentum, possibility, and support; avoid generic institutional messaging.
- Keep copy specific and credible using concrete proof points (outcomes, access, resources, examples).
- Maintain one primary message per piece when possible.

## Priority Audiences

- In-state prospective students
- Out-of-state prospective students
- Families and decision influencers
- Prospects in Utah County, rural Utah, current markets, and emerging recruitment markets

Tailor message emphasis by audience needs while preserving one unified campaign identity.

## Core Messaging Themes

Use these recurring proof directions when relevant:

- Financial aid and practical affordability
- Access and support for diverse student backgrounds
- Simple application journey
- Career outcomes and practical skill-building
- Proximity to jobs and industry access
- Distinctive setting and community in Salt Lake City/Utah

## Voice and Tone

- Write naturally and clearly for broad audiences.
- Avoid insider acronyms and field-specific jargon.
- Balance bold inspiration with concrete usefulness.
- Favor direct, concise language over abstract claims.
- Use emotional narrative only when it still supports clarity and action.

### Headline guidance

- Build strong first-impression headlines with clear focus.
- Use campaign momentum framing ("rise", "next peak", "upward") without overusing it.
- Do not rely on overly technical climbing language.
- Mix size/weight/tracking intentionally to keep display typography dynamic.

## Message Architecture

- Build a message map: core value proposition -> pillars -> proof points.
- Confirm each proof point clearly supports a pillar and the core proposition.
- Adjust proof points by department, audience, and channel while keeping core proposition intact.

## Visual System

## Color

- Lead with primary palette: Utah Red, Black, White.
- Target balance: approximately 75% primary palette, 25% secondary accents.
- Use secondary palette sparingly to add energy without overpowering primary identity.
- For digital, use RGB/HEX values intended for screen rendering.
- For print/physical, use official CMYK/SPOT workflows and official Utah Red sample matching.
- Verify accessible contrast (WCAG AA) before release.

## Typography

- Use campaign typefaces available through Adobe Fonts.
- Use the primary display face for impact headlines.
- Use the body face for legible, practical reading.
- Build hierarchy with deliberate contrast in size, weight, and spacing.

## Layout and shapes

- Use sharp corners and bevel logic; avoid rounded-corner defaults.
- Use 45-degree angle motifs inspired by terrain/block forms.
- Use mountain/ridgeline linework and dotted patterns as supporting devices.
- Keep composition purposeful and avoid decorative overload.

## Buttons and CTAs

- Use sharp-corner button styling in digital applications.
- Keep CTA copy clear and direct for the target audience.
- Ensure CTA contrast and hierarchy are obvious in the layout.

## Logo and Badge

- Follow university logo rules except campaign-specific overrides called out by campaign guidance.
- Keep clear space rules compatible with campaign bevel geometry when needed.
- Use campaign badge variants only in approved colors (White, Utah Red, Red Rocks, Black).
- Include the university logo alongside badge use in standard applications.

## Texture, Patterns, and Icons

- Use dotted/halftone textures to convey rugged determination and motion.
- Animate/build patterns in ways that suggest gearing up and upward progress.
- Build icons with simple geometric forms, mostly straight lines and sharp corners.
- Keep icon styling consistent and color usage aligned with palette rules.

## Photography and Video

## Photography

- Prioritize storytelling imagery that reflects people, place, and upward journey.
- Use surprising physical perspectives to reinforce directional campaign concept.
- Capture expressive faces and authentic moments.
- Pair portrait images with "you/your" language to improve identification and relevance.
- Use only approved asset libraries for final work.

## Motion and video

- Apply motion with clear purpose; do not distract from core message or accessibility.
- Use consistent timing/easing across assets.
- Separate productive motion (informational) from expressive motion (energy/brand feel).
- Use lower-thirds and consistent end slates where appropriate.
- Keep footage mostly true-to-life color; use high contrast/desaturation only when style calls for it.

## Channel Notes

- Social: combine upward narrative, concise message, and integrated graphic system.
- Email/web: prioritize scannability and top-load key information and CTA.
- Print collateral: emphasize tactile hierarchy, strong headline system, and narrative sequencing.
- Templates: use campaign-branded templates only for prospective-student-facing communications.

## Preflight QA Checklist

- Does the piece clearly align with core value proposition?
- Is the audience-specific need obvious in the copy?
- Is there one dominant message and one clear CTA?
- Are proof points concrete and credible?
- Does tone feel purposeful, dynamic, welcoming, and emboldening?
- Are color, type, and shape rules followed?
- Are contrast/accessibility checks passed?
- Are logo/badge applications compliant?
- Are imagery and motion choices on-brand and approved?
